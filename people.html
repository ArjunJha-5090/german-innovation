Scientists & Engineers

Johannes Gutenberg

Inventor of the printing press (c.1400–1468)

Invented movable-type printing press around 1440 in Mainz. His Gutenberg Bible revolutionized knowledge dissemination, education, and the spread of ideas across Europe.

Key Achievements:

  • Invented movable metal type printing (1440)
  • Printed the Gutenberg Bible (1455) - first major book
  • Made books affordable and accessible

Impact:

Sparked the Renaissance, Reformation, and Scientific Revolution. Literacy rates increased from 10% to 60% in 200 years.

Carl Benz

Automobile pioneer (1844–1929)

Patented the Benz Patent-Motorwagen in 1886, the world's first production automobile. Founded Benz & Cie., which later merged to form Mercedes-Benz.

Key Achievements:

  • Patent DRP 37435 (1886) - first automobile
  • Three-wheeled Motorwagen with 0.75 HP engine
  • Founded Benz & Cie. (1883)

Impact:

Started the automotive industry. His wife Bertha's 1888 road trip proved the car's viability, leading to mass adoption.

Wilhelm Conrad Röntgen

Physicist (1845–1923)

Discovered X-rays in 1895 at the University of Würzburg. Awarded the first Nobel Prize in Physics (1901). His discovery transformed medical diagnostics.

Rudolf Diesel

Engineer (1858–1913)

Invented the diesel engine in the 1890s. His compression-ignition engine became fundamental to transportation, shipping, and industry worldwide.

Albert Einstein

Theoretical Physicist (1879–1955)

Developed the theory of relativity (E=mc²). Nobel Prize in Physics (1921). His work reshaped our understanding of space, time, and energy.

Key Achievements:

  • Special Relativity (1905) - E=mc²
  • General Relativity (1915) - gravity as spacetime curvature
  • Photoelectric effect - Nobel Prize 1921
  • Brownian motion - proved atoms exist

Impact:

Most influential physicist of 20th century. Enabled GPS, nuclear energy, and modern cosmology.

Werner von Siemens

Electrical Engineer (1816–1892)

Founded Siemens AG. Invented the electric generator and developed the first electric elevator. Key figure in electrification of industry.

Max Planck

Physicist (1858–1947)

Father of quantum theory. Introduced Planck's constant. Nobel Prize in Physics (1918). His work laid the foundation for modern quantum mechanics.

Konrad Zuse

Computer Pioneer (1910–1995)

Built the Z3 (1941), the world's first programmable computer. Developed the first high-level programming language (Plankalkül).

Ferdinand Porsche

Automotive Engineer (1875–1951)

Designed the Volkswagen Beetle and founded Porsche. His engineering philosophy emphasized performance, efficiency, and innovation.
